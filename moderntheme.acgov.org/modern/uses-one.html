Sub-Menu
  • First Set of Examples
  • Second Set of Examples

That set of links on the left is what we call a "sub-menu" in web design jargon. These come in handy when we have more than two layers of content. You'll notice that there's a drop-down in the top navigation bar, which allows us to cover two layer of content. While it's not ideal from a usability standpoint to go much deeper than that, sometimes, you gotta! So, we use things like sub-menus, which allow us to keep the content organized into bite-sized chunks without cluttering up the top-level navigation.

Keep scrolling to see some variations of methods you can use to display your content. Then, go ahead and make another selection from the menu, to see even more!

Featured Content

Here we have another way to feature content. With this component you can use an icon or an image (example below) to highlight the theme of your content block or the website as a whole.

You can alternate each row left- or right- aligned as yet another method to break up your content into bite-sized pieces. You might choose to highlight people or services within your organization. 

Another Feature

This time we decided to use an image to highlight this row of content. Note that best practice would be consistent use of either icons or images - we're only using both so you can see what each looks like. For the most part, you'll want to stick with one look throughout the site, or per page. This will keep the design consistent, and your users happily scrolling!

Remember to keep your tab titles short, sweet, and to-the-point. Things can start to look a little less-than-appealing when the title drags on.
